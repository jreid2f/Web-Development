Tuning a Snare Drum

Home About Me Parts TUNING Drum Form

  1. If your drum doesn't sound right, then you need to clean up the drum a bit.
  2. Use a Drum Key in order to loosen or tighten the tension rods.
  3. Loosening the tension rods will allow you to have an easier time fine tuning the snare drum
  4. You should take the head of the drum off first and clean the inside of the shell of the drum.
  5. Once done cleaning, then put the head back on and tighten the tension rods as much as you need too.
  6. Each part of the head next to the tension rod needs to sound exactly the same all the way around.
  7. Once the Drum sounds like the same tone all around, then grab a stick and start playing.

Note: If you don't know what some of these parts are, then go to the "Parts" page on the website.
